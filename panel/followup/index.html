Panel Tool · Follow-Up

Schedule the Check-Ins

After an intervention or Trailback Panel, the work continues. This tool logs the record to Supabase and sends Outlook calendar events to your follow-up team — snapped to the nearest workday, timed to the student's grade schedule.

📌 Student & Intervention
Grade determines the calendar event time window — events land during a period that works for that student's schedule.
📝 Outcome Summary
Write a brief description of what occurred, what was decided, and what the student committed to. This text appears in every calendar event body so the team has context when each check-in arrives.
🗺️ Check-In Guidelines
These guidelines are embedded in every calendar event — so whoever shows up knows what the meeting is for.
🌱
2-Day Check-In · Early Pulse
Keep it brief and relational — this is not a consequence review. Ask: "How are you doing since we last talked?" and "Is there anything still unresolved?" Look for re-engagement and early signs of follow-through on any commitments. Reinforce their capacity to grow. 5–10 minutes max.
📋
2-Week Review · First Assessment
Look at the whole picture: grades, attendance, relationships with staff and peers. First real opportunity to assess whether the intervention is holding. Ask: "What's been hard? What's gotten easier?" Review any repair commitments; update the plan if needed. 10–20 minutes.
🏔️
2-Month Follow-Up · Long-Term Accountability
Pull the data: attendance, referrals, academic progress. Has the behavior pattern changed? Has the relationship with the community been repaired? Celebrate authentic growth. Determine if additional support — Camp, BIC, another panel — is needed. Document the outcome in Supabase. 15–30 minutes.
👥 Calendar Invitees
Each person receives Outlook calendar events for all three check-ins. Add everyone who should have accountability for this student's follow-up.
